# AAA Screening and Measurement Protocol

## Purpose

This synthetic protocol describes how abdominal aortic aneurysm (AAA) screening and measurement are represented in the NIH Research MCP demo. It is intended for mock research workflows only and is not clinical guidance.

## Eligibility

Patients are considered eligible for screening review when an abdominal CT study includes the infrarenal abdominal aorta and has sufficient image quality for diameter measurement. The synthetic cohort includes adults with both contrast and non-contrast CT examinations.

## Measurement method

Measure the maximum infrarenal abdominal aortic diameter using an outer-wall to outer-wall convention. Measurements should be made perpendicular to the vessel centerline when multiplanar reconstruction is available. If only axial review is available, record the largest short-axis diameter visible on contiguous slices.

## AAA threshold

For this demo, AAA-positive status is assigned when the maximum abdominal aortic diameter is greater than or equal to 3.0 cm. Borderline values near 3.0 cm should be flagged for reviewer confirmation in real research workflows.

## Reporting fields

Record patient identifier, age, sex, scan date, contrast status, scanner manufacturer, slice thickness, maximum AAA diameter, and whether the diameter meets the AAA-positive threshold.

## Quality control

Measurements above 5.5 cm or measurements that change by more than 0.5 cm between nearby studies should receive secondary review. Synthetic examples are deliberately simplified and should not be interpreted as patient care recommendations.
